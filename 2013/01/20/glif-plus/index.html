Glif Plus

Jan 20th, 2013

I enjoy being our unofficial family photographer. Since the birth of our first son almost three years ago, I’ve shot about a terabyte of images and video of our family. Recently, I’ve been going through our Aperture library, culling duplicates and deleting bad images, and I’ve come to realize two things about the video I’ve shot.

First, being the unofficial family photographer, I am in virtually none of the videos or pictures. Now, I don’t really mind, but I’m sure that my children will want to look back at this footage and see a more complete picture of everyone that was there.

Second, I would really like a way to capture longer videos of family moments. Even an uneventful dinner on a randon Tuesday night will surely be precious footage 30 years from now.

So, to address these two issues, I wanted a way to mount the iPhone on a standard tripod. I settled on the Glif Plus by Studio Neat. My plan is to leave a tripod in a corner nearby, always at-the-ready.

I’ve spent a few days with it, and so far I quite like it. All the pieces that come in contact with the iPhone are firm, non-scratchy rubber. Also, it feels secure on the tripod; I don’t worry that it will fall off.

Also, it’s pretty quick to setup. One corner of the phone slides into the rubber base, and then the other piece secures everything together.

Now, when I get home from work, I get to co-star in some home movies along with my wife and kids. Also, as an added bonus, my phone is now much less likely to be dividing my attention when I should be 100% focused on my family.
