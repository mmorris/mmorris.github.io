Children's Laboratory

Sep 26th, 2011

When I was young, I had a “laboratory” setup in the bedroom that I shared with my brother. We had microscopes, a telescope, chemistry sets, lots of magnets, and an electronics kit for kids. We tried to make smoke bombs with the chemistry set and made a crude alarm system for our bedroom door.

I was reminded of this setup during a recent conversation with Eric. His idea was to create a “workroom” where the kids could do things… a place where they could experiment, create, design, and pursue their interests.

I intend to steal his idea.

When my wife and I are finally able to build our dream home, I want to include just just a room strictly dedicated to tinkering. Here are a few projects that I’d like to do with them.

  • Ariel Photography (something like this)
  • Robotocs
  • Leatherworking (DIY toolbelts and sheaths)
  • Lockpicking (an essential skill if there ever was one)
  • Rube Goldberg machines
  • DIY telescope
  • DIY ultrasound (I think it can be done)
  • Ultra high speed photography
  • Could we build our own microwave oven? And here’s a rough list of some equipment that we could keep on hand.
  • Microscope
  • Telescope
  • computers
  • Arc welder
  • Soldering iron
  • Assorted tools
  • Chemistry set
  • Ham radios
  • Old clocks. The kind with gears, not circuits. Good for tinkering.
  • Lasers
  • Lenses
  • Oxy acetylene torch
  • Lock picking kit (I already have a set of lock picking tools)
  • Lots of old padlocks
  • A quality DSLR
  • First aid kit
  • Fire extinguisher

This is just off the cuff of my sleeve; who knows what the kids will dream up.
