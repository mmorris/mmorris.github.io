Hosting a Naked Domain on Amazon S3

Dec 28th, 2012

A few months ago I experimented with hosting this website on Amazon S3. It’s trivial to setup an s3 bucket as a website, and ponyHost makes it a breeze to deploy an Octopress website to the bucket. Static HTML/CSS + S3 hosting make for a very snappy website. However, at that time there was no clean way to use S3 to host a root domain, so I abandoned the effort.

Then just this morning, Amazon announced support for root domains with Amazon S3 hosted websites.

I decided to give it a try during lunch. I followed these instructions and in less than an hour I was all finished, save for DNS propagation. This site is now hosted on Amazon’s lightning-fast infrastructure. And if I need to, I can fail back to my shared hosting provider by simply changing the DNS nameservers at my registrar.

One lingering concern I have is cost. Although S3 prices are very economical under normal circumstances, the “Data Transfer Out” component of the pricing means that my monthly bill’s theoretical maximum is effectively infinite. Expensive accidents involving Amazon S3 are not without precedent. Even a single individual with an axe to grind and a 10 megabit home internet connection could do non-trivial damage to my wallet over the course of a month.

I’m sure there are ways to configure logging and/or notifications in a manner that would let me guard against such accidents or attacks, but I have not explored this yet. I’ll take a look at that over the weekend. In the meantime, please let me know if you happen to see any issue with the site.
