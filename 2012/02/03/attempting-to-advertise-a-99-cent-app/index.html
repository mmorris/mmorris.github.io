Attempting to Advertise a 99 Cent App

Feb 3rd, 2012

Over the holidays, I wrote an app for iPhone and iPad and published it on the App Store. It’s a simple video player for kids; definitely a niche market. Recently it occurred to me that I might reach out to new customers using online advertising.

I tried Google Adwords and Facebook Ads. Here are a few quick impressions.

Google Adwords

Adwords offers lots of keyword and targeting options. I find it a bit expensive for a $.99 app. I’m still trying to figure out how to get a Cost Per Click that will actually make me a profit.

After more than two weeks of waiting for my ad to be reviewed, and fixing problems they find with it, my ad still isn’t live anywhere. Not encouraging.

Facebook

Facebook’s interface is much simpler. After I created my first ad, it was approved and went live in less than an hour. This part of the experience was far superior to Google.

Also, their targeting system is a little different. Facebook must have a richer picture of its users than Google does. You can target by location, age, sex, marital status, education level, etc.

For my app, I chose to target parents with children under three years of age.

Results

Anyway, after about 45,000 impressions and 28 clicks, I had burned through the $10 that I had allocated to this experiment. These 28 clicks didn’t have a material effect on my sales for the day. I have since tightened my targeting; I’m getting fewer impressions/clicks, but hopefully they will better convert to sales.

So far, it doesn’t seem practical to advertise a $.99 app using Facebook or Google unless you make the effort to fine tune your ad’s text, keywords, and targeting. This is a process that might take weeks of tweaking and A/B testing. And for certain niche apps, it might not even be possible to effectively target your audience.
